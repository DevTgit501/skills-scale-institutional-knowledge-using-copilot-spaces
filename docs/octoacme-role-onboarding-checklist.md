# OctoAcme Role Onboarding Checklist

This checklist helps teams effectively onboard new team members into their specific roles. Use the role-specific checklist for each new team member joining a project.

---

## General Onboarding (All Roles)

Complete these items for any new team member:

### Week 1: Orientation
- [ ] **Welcome & Schedule** - Set up calendar invites for all ceremonies and meetings
- [ ] **Access & Tools** - Provision accounts (GitHub, Jira, Slack, documentation systems, etc.)
- [ ] **Meet the Team** - Schedule 1:1s with key collaborators
- [ ] **Company/Project Context** - Share organizational vision and project charter
- [ ] **Documentation** - Provide links to OctoAcme personas, process docs, and project runbooks
- [ ] **Assigned Buddy** - Pair with experienced team member for ongoing guidance
- [ ] **First Attendance** - Attend first standup, sprint planning, and team meeting

### Week 2-3: Role Immersion
- [ ] **Role Deep Dive** - Review role-specific responsibilities and RACI matrix
- [ ] **Attend Ceremonies** - Observe/participate in sprint planning, standups, retrospectives
- [ ] **Key Stakeholders** - Meet with Product Manager, Technical Lead, or other key partners
- [ ] **Tools Training** - Deep dive on project management tools, tracking systems
- [ ] **Communication Norms** - Understand team communication patterns and expectations
- [ ] **Initial Task** - Take on small, guided task to practice role with mentoring

### Week 4+: Ramp Up
- [ ] **Increased Ownership** - Take on tasks with decreasing oversight
- [ ] **Cross-Functional Work** - Collaborate with other roles (attend code reviews, customer calls, etc.)
- [ ] **Documentation Updates** - Contribute to process improvement documentation
- [ ] **Blocker Resolution** - Start identifying and helping resolve team blockers
- [ ] **Feedback Survey** - Provide feedback on onboarding experience
- [ ] **30-Day Check-in** - Formal check-in with manager/lead on ramp-up progress

## Developer Onboarding

### Pre-Start (1 week before arrival)
- [ ] **Development Setup** - Prepare development environment, access, and tools
- [ ] **Repository Access** - Grant access to code repos and deployment systems
- [ ] **Code Review Setup** - Add to code review processes and PR templates
- [ ] **CI/CD Walkthrough** - Document build/test/deploy pipeline
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced developer for guidance

### Week 1: Foundations
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Repository Tour** - Walk through codebase structure, architecture overview
- [ ] **Development Environment** - Verify local setup, build, and test processes work
- [ ] **First Build** - Successfully build and run application locally
- [ ] **Coding Standards** - Review team's coding standards and practices
- [ ] **Testing Approach** - Understand unit testing, integration testing, and test coverage expectations

### Week 2-3: Active Development
- [ ] **First PR** - Submit first pull request with mentor review
- [ ] **Code Review Practice** - Participate in code reviews both as reviewer and reviewee
- [ ] **Architecture Discussion** - Attend design review or architecture discussion
- [ ] **Documentation Review** - Read relevant API docs, technical design docs
- [ ] **Bug Fix** - Complete first bug fix and deploy to staging/production
- [ ] **Blocker Support** - Help resolve team technical blockers

### Week 4+: Full Participation
- [ ] **Feature Development** - Lead development on larger feature areas
- [ ] **Technical Mentoring** - Help mentor other developers or share knowledge
- [ ] **Performance Optimization** - Contribute to performance or scalability improvements
- [ ] **Code Quality** - Take ownership of code quality in assigned areas
- [ ] **Knowledge Sharing** - Lead tech talk or document technical learning

### Success Criteria
✓ Developer can:
- Understand and navigate the codebase independently
- Submit PRs that pass code review on first or second iteration
- Debug and fix issues without significant assistance
- Help unblock other team members on technical problems
- Attend and participate meaningfully in standups and design reviews

## Product Manager Onboarding

### Pre-Start (2 weeks before arrival)
- [ ] **Product Context** - Share product roadmap, competitive landscape, user research
- [ ] **Stakeholder List** - Provide list of key stakeholders, customers, and internal partners
- [ ] **Market Research** - Share relevant market analysis and customer feedback
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced PM for ongoing guidance

### Week 1: Immersion
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Product History** - Review product evolution, past decisions, lessons learned
- [ ] **Customer Interviews** - Schedule 2-3 customer interviews to understand user needs
- [ ] **Competitive Analysis** - Review competitive positioning and differentiation
- [ ] **Backlog Review** - Deep dive on current backlog, priorities, and in-flight work
- [ ] **Metrics Dashboard** - Get access to product metrics, KPIs, and data dashboards

### Week 2-3: Planning & Strategy
- [ ] **Roadmap Planning** - Participate in roadmap refinement or planning discussions
- [ ] **Acceptance Criteria** - Review and help refine acceptance criteria for upcoming work
- [ ] **Stakeholder Meetings** - Attend stakeholder reviews or quarterly business reviews
- [ ] **Prioritization Exercise** - Practice prioritization with mentor feedback
- [ ] **Feature Definition** - Write or lead PRD (Product Requirements Document) for a small feature
- [ ] **Customer Feedback** - Synthesize customer feedback and discuss implications

### Week 4+: Full Ownership
- [ ] **Backlog Ownership** - Own prioritization and refinement of product backlog
- [ ] **Requirement Definition** - Lead definition of new features and initiatives
- [ ] **Cross-Functional Leadership** - Drive decisions across product, engineering, customer success
- [ ] **Customer Advocacy** - Represent customer needs in technical and business discussions
- [ ] **Metrics Tracking** - Monitor and review product metrics and success indicators
- [ ] **Go-to-Market** - Lead launch planning or release planning for key features

### Success Criteria
✓ Product Manager can:
- Articulate product vision, strategy, and roadmap clearly
- Prioritize backlog based on customer value and business objectives
- Work effectively with engineering and stakeholders to align on priorities
- Define clear acceptance criteria and success metrics
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback for product improvements

## Project Manager Onboarding

### Pre-Start (2 weeks before arrival)
- [ ] **Project Charter** - Review project scope, timeline, stakeholders, and risks
- [ ] **Team Composition** - Understand team members, skills, and availability
- [ ] **Stakeholder List** - List of stakeholders, approval authorities, and communication preferences
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced PM for guidance

### Week 1: Project Foundations
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Project Plan Review** - Review existing project plans, timelines, milestones
- [ ] **Risk Register** - Understand current risks, mitigations, and monitoring
- [ ] **Team Meetings** - Attend first team planning and status meetings
- [ ] **Stakeholder Alignment** - Meet with key stakeholders for priorities and expectations
- [ ] **Communication Plan** - Understand reporting cadence and communication preferences

### Week 2-3: Active Management
- [ ] **Planning Session** - Lead or co-lead sprint planning session
- [ ] **Risk Assessment** - Conduct risk identification and assessment exercise
- [ ] **Status Reporting** - Prepare and deliver first status report to stakeholders
- [ ] **Schedule Management** - Work with team to create/update detailed project schedule
- [ ] **Dependency Mapping** - Identify and document cross-team dependencies
- [ ] **Blocker Resolution** - Help remove team blockers and escalate as needed

### Week 4+: Full Ownership
- [ ] **Project Delivery** - Own project schedule, risks, and stakeholder communication
- [ ] **Decision Making** - Lead trade-off decisions and scope management
- [ ] **Team Coordination** - Facilitate effective cross-functional collaboration
- [ ] **Progress Tracking** - Accurately track progress against plan and adjust
- [ ] **Issue Resolution** - Escalate and drive resolution of project impediments
- [ ] **Retrospectives** - Lead project retrospectives and capture learnings

### Success Criteria
✓ Project Manager can:
- Understand and articulate project scope, timeline, and success criteria
- Facilitate effective team collaboration across functional areas
- Track progress and identify risks or schedule impacts early
- Manage stakeholder expectations and provide timely, accurate status updates
- Navigate and resolve project issues through escalation and decision-making

## QA/Quality Assurance Onboarding

### Pre-Start (1 week before arrival)
- [ ] **Test Infrastructure** - Prepare test environments and access to testing tools
- [ ] **Acceptance Criteria** - Share current feature specs and acceptance criteria
- [ ] **Known Issues** - Document known issues, bugs, and workarounds
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Pair with experienced QA or Senior Developer for guidance

### Week 1: Quality Foundations
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Test Strategy Review** - Understand testing approach (unit, integration, E2E, manual)
- [ ] **Test Environment Setup** - Access and configure test environments and devices
- [ ] **Product Features** - Demo of current product and key features
- [ ] **Bugs & Issues** - Review known bugs, defects, and QA status
- [ ] **Acceptance Criteria** - Review examples of well-defined acceptance criteria

### Week 2-3: Test Case Development
- [ ] **First Test Cases** - Write initial test cases with mentor feedback
- [ ] **Test Plan Review** - Review example test plans with technical lead or QA mentor
- [ ] **Environment Testing** - Test in staging and production-like environments
- [ ] **Bug Reporting** - Report first defects with clear reproduction steps
- [ ] **Test Automation** - Set up or run automated testing suite
- [ ] **Release Readiness** - Participate in pre-release testing and quality gate assessment

### Week 4+: Full QA Responsibility
- [ ] **Test Coverage** - Establish comprehensive test coverage for features
- [ ] **Defect Management** - Manage defect identification, tracking, and resolution
- [ ] **Quality Metrics** - Track and report testing metrics and quality status
- [ ] **Test Automation** - Contribute to test automation infrastructure improvements
- [ ] **Standards Enforcement** - Hold team accountable to quality standards and acceptance criteria
- [ ] **Release Validation** - Lead pre-release quality gate and validation

### Success Criteria
✓ QA Engineer can:
- Understand product features and use cases thoroughly
- Write clear test cases tied to acceptance criteria
- Identify and document defects with clear reproduction information
- Assess release readiness based on test coverage and quality metrics
- Collaborate effectively with developers to resolve quality issues

## Technical Lead Onboarding

### Pre-Start (2 weeks before arrival)
- [ ] **Codebase Overview** - Provide architecture diagrams and technical documentation
- [ ] **Past Decisions** - Share ADRs (Architecture Decision Records) or tech strategy docs
- [ ] **Technical Roadmap** - Technology and infrastructure improvements planned
- [ ] **Team Expertise** - Profile of developer skills and technical gaps
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced architect or senior tech lead

### Week 1: Technical Deep Dive
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Architecture Review** - Comprehensive architecture overview and rationale
- [ ] **Codebase Walkthrough** - Guided tour of codebase structure and key components
- [ ] **Technical Debt** - Review current technical debt, scalability concerns
- [ ] **Development Practices** - Code review process, standards, testing approach
- [ ] **Technology Stack** - Review frameworks, libraries, and tools in use

### Week 2-3: Leadership Activities
- [ ] **Code Review** - Participate in code reviews and provide technical guidance
- [ ] **Design Discussion** - Attend or lead architecture design discussion
- [ ] **Technical Risk Assessment** - Review and assess technical risks in backlog
- [ ] **Developer Mentoring** - Provide technical mentoring to one or more developers
- [ ] **Technical Standards** - Review and help refine coding standards
- [ ] **Performance Analysis** - Review performance bottlenecks or optimization opportunities

### Week 4+: Full Technical Leadership
- [ ] **Architecture Decisions** - Lead architectural decisions for new initiatives
- [ ] **Technical Strategy** - Guide long-term technical direction and roadmap
- [ ] **Quality Oversight** - Ensure code quality and architectural integrity
- [ ] **Technical Mentoring** - Mentor developers on best practices and advanced topics
- [ ] **Risk Management** - Identify technical risks and drive mitigation
- [ ] **Innovation** - Evaluate and recommend emerging technologies and approaches

### Success Criteria
✓ Technical Lead can:
- Understand system architecture and design rationale
- Guide technical decisions aligned with strategy and best practices
- Mentor developers and help them improve technical capabilities
- Identify technical risks and drive appropriate mitigation
- Advocate for technical quality while balancing business needs

## DevOps/Release Engineer Onboarding

### Pre-Start (2 weeks before arrival)
- [ ] **Infrastructure Docs** - Provide infrastructure architecture and documentation
- [ ] **CI/CD Pipeline** - Share pipeline configuration and deployment procedures
- [ ] **Incident History** - Review past incidents, lessons learned, and improvements
- [ ] **Access & Tools** - Prepare AWS/cloud access, deployment tools, monitoring systems
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced DevOps engineer or infrastructure lead

### Week 1: Infrastructure Foundations
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Infrastructure Tour** - Review infrastructure architecture and components
- [ ] **Environment Setup** - Verify access to dev, staging, and production environments
- [ ] **CI/CD Pipeline** - Understand build, test, and deployment automation
- [ ] **Monitoring & Alerts** - Review monitoring, alerting, and incident response setup
- [ ] **Security Policies** - Review infrastructure security, access controls, compliance

### Week 2-3: Operational Tasks
- [ ] **Deployment Support** - Support a deployment with hands-on guidance
- [ ] **Log Analysis** - Review logs and monitoring data from recent deployments
- [ ] **Configuration Changes** - Make small, low-risk configuration or environment changes
- [ ] **Automation Improvements** - Identify automation improvements or gaps
- [ ] **Incident Response** - Participate in incident investigation and response
- [ ] **Documentation Review** - Review and update deployment runbooks

### Week 4+: Full DevOps Responsibility
- [ ] **Deployment Leadership** - Lead deployment activities and rollback if needed
- [ ] **Infrastructure Changes** - Design and implement infrastructure improvements
- [ ] **Performance Optimization** - Optimize for performance, cost, and reliability
- [ ] **Incident Management** - Lead incident response and post-mortems
- [ ] **Monitoring/Alerting** - Improve observability and alerting for early detection
- [ ] **Disaster Recovery** - Lead planning and testing of DR and failover procedures

### Success Criteria
✓ DevOps Engineer can:
- Navigate infrastructure and understand deployment automation
- Execute deployments confidently and safely
- Troubleshoot infrastructure issues and performance problems
- Respond to and help resolve incidents
- Continuously improve deployment processes and infrastructure reliability

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach Onboarding

### Pre-Start (1 week before arrival)
- [ ] **Team Profile** - Understand team size, agile maturity, and challenges
- [ ] **Process Documentation** - Provide current sprint structure and ceremony format
- [ ] **Team Dynamics** - Information on team composition and working relationships
- [ ] **Metric Tracking** - Share current velocity, burndown, and team metrics
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced Scrum Master for guidance

### Week 1: Team & Process Immersion
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Observe Ceremonies** - Attend all standups, planning, reviews, retrospectives
- [ ] **Team Meeting** - Meet individually with each team member (10-15 min conversations)
- [ ] **Team Dynamics** - Understand working relationships, conflicts, collaboration patterns
- [ ] **Current Challenges** - Ask team about biggest process challenges and blockers
- [ ] **Metrics Review** - Review team velocity, burndown, and retrospective notes

### Week 2-3: Facilitation
- [ ] **Sprint Planning** - Co-facilitate or lead sprint planning
- [ ] **Daily Standups** - Facilitate daily standups and capture blockers
- [ ] **Sprint Review** - Facilitate sprint review/demo with focused engagement
- [ ] **Retrospective** - Plan and facilitate retrospective focused on improvements
- [ ] **Blocker Resolution** - Help team identify and resolve blockers
- [ ] **Metrics Tracking** - Track and communicate velocity, sprint health metrics

### Week 4+: Full Agile Coaching
- [ ] **Ceremony Leadership** - Own all sprint ceremonies and quality of facilitation
- [ ] **Team Coaching** - Coach team on agile principles and practices
- [ ] **Process Improvement** - Identify and pilot process improvements based on feedback
- [ ] **Collaboration** - Work with Product Manager and Project Manager on sprint scope
- [ ] **Engagement** - Help maintain team engagement and psychological safety
- [ ] **Continuous Improvement** - Lead team's continuous improvement journey

### Success Criteria
✓ Scrum Master can:
- Facilitate effective, focused ceremonies
- Help team identify blockers and work to remove impediments
- Build psychological safety and encourage open feedback
- Understand team dynamics and communication patterns
- Drive continuous process improvement based on team feedback

## Customer Success Manager Onboarding

### Pre-Start (2 weeks before arrival)
- [ ] **Customer List** - List of Key accounts and their success metrics
- [ ] **Product Knowledge** - Share product documentation and feature roadmap
- [ ] **Customer Feedback** - Recent customer interviews, feedback, NPS data
- [ ] **Success Metrics** - Define key customer success metrics and health indicators
- [ ] **Mentor Assignment** - Assign experienced CSM or account manager

### Week 1: Customer & Product Immersion
- [ ] **General Onboarding** (see above)
- [ ] **Product Deep Dive** - Hands-on product walkthrough and feature training
- [ ] **Customer Interviews** - Conduct 3-5 customer interviews
- [ ] **Customer Data** - Review customer health metrics, usage data, churn risk
- [ ] **Onboarding Process** - Review customer onboarding and adoption process
- [ ] **Feedback Channels** - Understand how customer feedback flows to product

### Week 2-3: Customer Engagement
- [ ] **First Touchpoint** - Owner of onboarding call for a new or at-risk customer
- [ ] **Health Assessment** - Assess health and adoption of assigned customers
- [ ] **Feedback Synthesis** - Summarize customer feedback for product/PM discussion
- [ ] **Training Delivery** - Deliver product training or feature walkthrough to customer
- [ ] **Success Planning** - Work with customer to define success metrics
- [ ] **Adoption Support** - Help customer get value from product features

### Week 4+: Full Customer Ownership
- [ ] **Account Management** - Own customer health and outcomes for assigned accounts
- [ ] **Proactive Outreach** - Regularly check in with customers on health and outcomes
- [ ] **Escalation** - Identify and escalate customer issues or churn risks early
- [ ] **Feature Advocacy** - Represent customer needs in product roadmap discussions
- [ ] **Expansion** - Identify and pursue expansion opportunities
- [ ] **Success Stories** - Develop and share customer success stories

### Success Criteria
✓ Customer Success Manager can:
- Understand customer use cases, success metrics, and business objectives
- Assess customer health and adoption levels
- Proactively help customers achieve their goals
- Gather actionable customer feedback for product improvement
- Identify and help prevent customer churn through engagement

## 30-Day Onboarding Review

At the end of 30 days, conduct a check-in:

### For New Team Member
- [ ] **Progress Check** - How do you feel about progress in the role?
- [ ] **Confidence Level** - What areas are you confident in? Where do you need more support?
- [ ] **Feedback** - What went well in onboarding? What could have been better?
- [ ] **Next Steps** - What are you focusing on in month 2?
- [ ] **Onboarding Improvements** - Suggestions to improve onboarding for future hires?

### For Manager/Lead
- [ ] **Ramp Progress** - How is the team member ramping compared to expected timeline?
- [ ] **Skill Gaps** - Are there skill gaps or areas that need additional focus?
- [ ] **Team Fit** - How is the team member fitting with the current team?
- [ ] **Support Needs** - What additional support or mentoring would help?
- [ ] **Confidence Level** - Are you confident in their independent contribution?

### Team Integration
- [ ] **Celebrate Progress** - Acknowledge progress and contributions made
- [ ] **Adjust Support** - Increase autonomy while maintaining mentoring as needed
- [ ] **Extend Onboarding** - Plan ongoing development beyond initial 30 days
- [ ] **Share Success** - Share learnings from onboarding with team
