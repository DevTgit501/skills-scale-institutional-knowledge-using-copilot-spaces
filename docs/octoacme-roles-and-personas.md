# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Quality Assurance Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers ensure quality standards, validate acceptance criteria, and identify defects early in the development cycle. They are critical quality gatekeepers who collaborate with Developers to deliver reliable products.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Perform manual and automated testing across features and releases
- Identify, document, and track defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate with Developers to resolve quality issues
- Validate release readiness and execute deployment tests
- Maintain and improve testing infrastructure and automation
- Participate in sprint planning to understand testing requirements

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high product quality and user experience
- Enable faster feedback loops through efficient testing
- Reduce time spent on manual, repetitive testing

### Typical Communication
- Test status updates in standups and sprint reviews
- Defect reports and test results with clear acceptance criteria
- Pre-release quality gate updates
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test case design, debug failures, and clarify acceptance criteria
- **Product Manager**: Clarify acceptance criteria and prioritize testing efforts
- **Project Manager**: Provide status updates on test coverage and release readiness

---

## Technical Lead/Architecture Owner

### Role Summary
Technical Leads make architectural decisions, provide technical strategy, and mentor developers on best practices. They balance innovation with maintainability and ensure systems remain scalable.

### Responsibilities
- Guide architectural decisions and lead design reviews
- Ensure adherence to coding standards and technical best practices
- Mentor junior developers and provide technical guidance
- Identify and create plans for reducing technical debt
- Plan for system scalability, performance, and maintainability
- Evaluate and recommend technology choices
- Collaborate with Product Manager on technical feasibility and trade-offs

### Goals
- Create scalable, maintainable systems
- Build high-performing teams through mentoring
- Balance velocity with technical quality
- Prevent accumulation of technical debt

### Typical Communication
- Architecture design docs and RFCs (Request for Comments)
- Code review feedback and technical mentoring sessions
- Technical feasibility discussions with Product Manager
- Architecture discussions in sprint planning

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance, conduct architecture reviews, mentor on best practices
- **Product Manager**: Discuss technical feasibility and implementation options
- **Project Manager**: Communicate technical risks and dependencies
- **DevOps Engineer**: Collaborate on scalability and deployment architecture

---

## DevOps/Release Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment processes. They enable the team to release frequently and reliably.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage production infrastructure, environments, and capacity
- Coordinate release deployments and manage rollback procedures
- Monitor system health, incident response, and post-mortems
- Document deployment procedures and operational runbooks
- Collaborate with Developers on observability, logging, and monitoring
- Ensure infrastructure security and compliance

### Goals
- Enable safe, frequent deployments
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Maintain reliable, scalable, and secure infrastructure
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents

### Typical Communication
- Deployment readiness checklists and release notes
- Infrastructure and capacity planning updates
- Incident reports and post-mortem action items
- Monitoring and alerting configuration documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on deployability, logging, and monitoring
- **Project Manager**: Communicate deployment schedules and risks
- **Technical Lead**: Discuss scalability architecture and infrastructure design
- **QA Engineer**: Verify deployment procedures and production readiness

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on agile practices. They foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove blockers that impede team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles, practices, and mindset
- Track and communicate team velocity, sprint health, and metrics
- Promote psychological safety and encourage open communication
- Help the team reflect on processes and implement improvements
- Coordinate with Project Manager on sprint scope and planning

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and sustainable pace
- Foster continuous improvement and learning culture
- Maintain team morale, engagement, and autonomy
- Reduce cycle time and improve predictability

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies and facilitation notes
- Team health metrics and retrospective action items
- Blocker escalations and resolution tracking
- Coaching and feedback discussions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate on sprint planning and risk management
- **Developers**: Remove blockers and facilitate technical discussions
- **Product Manager**: Help clarify requirements during sprint planning
- **All Team Members**: Coach and support agile practice adoption

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes and gather feedback to inform product decisions. They are the bridge between customer needs and product development.

### Responsibilities
- Onboard new customers and ensure successful adoption
- Gather customer feedback, usage insights, and satisfaction metrics
- Identify and escalate critical customer issues and concerns
- Communicate product updates, features, and changes to customers
- Partner with Product Manager on feature priorities and roadmap
- Track customer health metrics, usage trends, and churn risks
- Advocate for customer needs in product planning discussions

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction, retention, and growth
- Generate actionable customer insights for product improvement
- Enable revenue growth through customer success and expansion
- Reduce customer churn and support issues

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and feature requests
- Adoption metrics and customer health reports
- Joint planning sessions with Product Manager
- Customer case studies and success stories

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Share customer feedback and collaborate on prioritization
- **Developers**: Communicate customer use cases and feature feedback
- **Project Manager**: Update on customer adoption progress and timelines
- **Project Manager**: Flag escalations and urgent customer issues

---

## Stakeholder/Executive Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders provide business context, strategic requirements, and oversight for projects. They ensure projects deliver business value and remain aligned with organizational strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives, success metrics, and desired outcomes
- Provide funding, resources, and organizational support
- Make prioritization and trade-off decisions
- Approve major releases, milestones, and scope changes
- Communicate project status to leadership and other stakeholders
- Remove organizational and cross-functional blockers
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain alignment between projects and strategy
- Enable efficient resource allocation and prioritization
- Support organizational change and capability building

### Typical Communication
- Weekly/monthly status reports and dashboards
- Milestone reviews and approvals
- Quarterly business reviews and strategy alignment
- Escalation and decision logs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Regular status updates and escalation handling
- **Product Manager**: Align on roadmap and business priorities
- **Executive Leadership**: Communicate outcomes and impact
- **All Team Members**: Provide context on business importance
